Make sure your pan is properly preheated before adding oil. Use enough oil to coat the surface, and don't move food too early. Let it develop a crust before flipping. For stainless steel, the water droplet test works great - if droplets dance, it's ready!

Rinse rice thoroughly before cooking to remove excess starch. Use the right water-to-rice ratio (usually 2:1 for white rice). Bring to a boil, then immediately reduce to low heat and cover. Let it rest covered for 10 minutes after cooking - this is crucial for fluffy texture!

Temperature shock is usually the culprit. When adding dairy to hot sauces, temper it first by adding a small amount of hot liquid to the dairy to warm it gradually. Then whisk continuously while slowly adding it to the hot sauce. Remove from heat before adding dairy when possible.
